Kawasaki surprised the entire side-by-side industry when it unveiled the Ridge in 2024. Not because it's just another capable utility UTV — but because it's the world's first full-time 4WD side-by-side ever built. While every other manufacturer in the segment toggles between 2WD and 4WD modes, the Ridge stays locked in four-wheel drive all the time. That single engineering decision changes how you drive, what you can carry, and — most importantly for this guide — what accessories make sense to add.

We've spent time reviewing the rapidly expanding ecosystem of Kawasaki Ridge UTV accessories, from Kawasaki's own OEM catalog to the aftermarket parts manufacturers who have rushed to support this new platform. This guide breaks down the best additions for work, recreation, weather protection, and trail performance.

Why the Kawasaki Ridge Demands a Different Accessories Strategy

Traditional UTV accessory buying is straightforward: pick a popular machine like the Polaris Ranger or Can-Am Defender, and you have a decade's worth of proven aftermarket parts to choose from. The Kawasaki Ridge changes that equation. As a brand-new platform from a brand-new entrant in the pure utility SxS space, Ridge owners are working with a smaller but quickly growing pool of accessories — and the choices you make matter more.

The Ridge's 999cc parallel-twin engine and full-time 4WD architecture mean it handles differently than switchable 4WD machines. It also means accessories that take advantage of consistent traction — like heavy cargo management systems, trailer hitches, and winch kits — are especially valuable investments.

Full Hard-Coat Polycarbonate Windshield

The first upgrade most Ridge owners make is a proper full windshield. The Ridge's cab structure is built for enclosure accessories, and a scratch-resistant, hard-coated polycarbonate windshield blocks dust, debris, rain, and wind without sacrificing forward visibility. Unlike glass options, poly windshields won't shatter on trail impacts — a major safety advantage.

Look for windshields with vented panels that allow fresh airflow on warm days without opening yourself to roost from the front wheels. Installation typically takes under an hour with basic hardware.

Cargo Bed Management System

The Kawasaki Ridge's rear cargo bed is one of its defining work features. A bed management system — including a cargo divider, tie-down anchors, and a fitted rubber mat — transforms the raw bed into a tool that holds gear securely through the Ridge's active 4WD maneuvers. Because the Ridge never disengages from 4WD, you can push harder through terrain, which means your cargo needs to stay put.

Heavy-duty bed extenders that mount to the receiver or bed rails can increase hauling capacity for long items like lumber, pipe, or camping gear without hanging over the tailgate unsecured.

Protection Accessories — Skids, Guards, and Armor

Full-time 4WD means you'll push into terrain that switchable 4WD operators might avoid. That makes underbody protection especially important on the Ridge. Skid plates for the engine, transmission, and fuel tank prevent costly damage from rocks and logs. Quality skid plate kits for the Ridge mount with existing frame bolts and add minimal weight while providing substantial protection.

Rock sliders and door guards are also early popular additions. The Ridge's entry-level trim runs open-cab, and adding aftermarket aluminum or steel door panels with integrated sliders protects both occupants and the frame rails from side impacts on tight trails.

Tire upgrades are another high-value mod. The stock tires on the Ridge are capable, but many owners swap to 27-inch or 28-inch aggressive all-terrain tires within the first season. The full-time 4WD system ensures even wear across all four corners when tire sizes are matched.

Comfort and Cab Accessories

Spending a full workday or weekend in the Ridge means comfort matters as much as performance. The cab accessory category has expanded quickly for this platform. Roof panels — hard or soft — provide sun and rain protection without the weight of a full cab enclosure. Rear panels and side nets convert the open cab into a semi-enclosed space suitable for cooler weather without sacrificing the open-air feel.

Seat covers specifically designed for UTV use protect the Ridge's factory seat upholstery from mud, sweat, and UV degradation. Adding a contoured foam seat cushion can also significantly reduce fatigue on long rides, particularly important for farmers and ranchers who spend 6–8 hours daily in the machine.

Steering wheel covers with vibration dampening reduce hand fatigue on rocky trails. UTV-specific steering wheel wraps are available in leather, neoprene, and mesh materials to suit preferences for feel and grip.

Towing and Hauling Accessories

With 2,500 lbs of towing capacity, the Kawasaki Ridge earns its utility credentials. But maximizing that towing potential requires the right accessories. A 2-inch receiver hitch adapter (if not factory-equipped on your trim) opens the door to a full range of trailer hitches, cargo carriers, and ball mounts. Towing mirrors that clamp to existing side mirrors improve visibility when pulling wide trailers.

Electric winches in the 3,000 to 4,500 lb range pair perfectly with the Ridge's frame-forward mounting points. The full-time 4WD means you can often self-recover without a winch, but having one adds serious peace of mind for solo work in remote terrain. Synthetic rope winches are lighter and safer than steel cable — they don't coil dangerously when they snap.

Communication and Electronics

Modern UTV riders want connectivity in the cab. RAM mounts and Quad-Lock style device holders keep phones and GPS units secure on the dash without blocking instrument visibility. The Ridge's electrical system supports USB charging accessories for keeping devices powered through long days.

Two-way radio mounts are essential for group riding or work crews spread across a property. Many UTV riders run dedicated GMRS radios with cab-mounted antennas for reliable communication beyond typical Bluetooth or cellular range.

Bluetooth speaker bars that mount to the ROPS (roll-over protection structure) or roof rack deliver music and navigation audio over engine noise without requiring helmet headsets. Look for IP67-rated waterproof models that handle trail dust and rain without issue.

Frequently Asked Questions — Kawasaki Ridge UTV Accessories

What makes the Kawasaki Ridge different from other UTVs?

The Kawasaki Ridge is the world's first full-time 4WD side-by-side. Unlike competitors that switch between 2WD and 4WD, the Ridge maintains 4WD traction constantly, giving it a unique advantage on unpredictable terrain.

What engine powers the Kawasaki Ridge?

The Kawasaki Ridge runs a 999cc parallel-twin engine producing approximately 100 horsepower, paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission designed for both trail and work duty.

What is the towing capacity of the Kawasaki Ridge?

The Kawasaki Ridge has a towing capacity of 2,500 lbs, making it competitive with the Can-Am Defender and Polaris Ranger for hauling trailers and implements.

Are OEM accessories available for the Kawasaki Ridge?

Yes. Kawasaki offers a growing lineup of OEM accessories through its 1,000+ US dealer network. Aftermarket options are also expanding rapidly as the platform matures.

What are the most popular Kawasaki Ridge UTV accessories?

Top accessories include full-cab enclosures, roof panels, windshields, LED light bars, cargo bed extenders, skid plates, and heavy-duty floor liners.

Does the Kawasaki Ridge need a windshield?

While the Ridge comes with a standard windshield option, many owners upgrade to a full hard-coated polycarbonate windshield for better protection against debris and weather.

What aftermarket seats work with the Kawasaki Ridge?

Several UTV seat manufacturers offer bolt-compatible seats for the Ridge. Adding seat cushions or full replacement sport seats can dramatically improve ride comfort on long days.

How does the Kawasaki Ridge perform off-road?

The full-time 4WD system gives the Ridge exceptional traction in mud, rock, and loose terrain without the driver needing to manually engage 4WD, making it easier to handle tricky situations on the fly.

Is the Kawasaki Ridge good for farm use?

Absolutely. The 2,500 lb tow capacity, 1,000 lb bed payload, and full-time 4WD make it an outstanding farm utility vehicle capable of handling daily agricultural work through all seasons.

Building Your Perfect Kawasaki Ridge Setup

The Kawasaki Ridge is a blank canvas for any buyer who prioritizes traction, reliability, and Kawasaki's legendary build quality. While the accessory ecosystem is younger than established platforms, it's growing fast — and the quality of available accessories is already impressive.

For most buyers, a logical build order looks like this: Start with protection (windshield, skid plates, brush guards), then add utility (bed management, towing accessories), then upgrade comfort (roof, cab panels, seat upgrades), and finally layer in electronics (lights, audio, communications). This order prioritizes function and protection before comfort, which is the right approach for a machine built to work.

Kawasaki's dealer network provides a solid resource for OEM parts and guidance, but don't overlook the growing independent accessory manufacturers who have already committed to the Ridge platform. Quality third-party options often deliver the same protection or performance at a more accessible price point, and the Ridge's compatibility with many standard UTV mounting systems means you have more choices than you might expect for such a new machine.
